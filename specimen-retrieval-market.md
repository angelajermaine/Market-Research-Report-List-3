<p><p>Specimen retrieval devices can be categorized into detachable and non-detachable types based on their design and functionality. Detachable devices allow for easy removal of the specimen from the surgical site, which can be advantageous for certain procedures. On the other hand, non-detachable devices are designed to securely hold the specimen in place until it is manually removed by the surgeon. Both types of devices play a crucial role in minimally invasive surgical procedures, providing surgeons with options to effectively retrieve specimens during the operation.</p></p>
