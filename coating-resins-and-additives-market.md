<p><p>Coating resins are materials used to form a continuous film on a surface, providing protection, adhesion, and aesthetics. They can be categorized into acrylic, alkyd, epoxy, polyurethane, and others. Coating additives are substances added to coatings to improve properties such as durability, flow control, UV resistance, and color retention. Examples include rheology modifiers, dispersants, and defoamers. Both the coating resins and additives markets are driven by the construction, automotive, and industrial sectors' demand for high-performance coatings.</p></p>
