<p><p>Dynamic orthotics are devices designed to provide continuous and adjustable support to the upper limb while allowing for movement and flexibility. They are typically used for conditions such as muscle weakness or nerve damage. Static orthotics, on the other hand, are rigid devices that provide stability and support to the upper limb without allowing for much movement. They are commonly used for conditions such as fractures or joint instability. Both types of orthotics play a crucial role in the treatment and rehabilitation of upper limb injuries and impairments.</p></p>
