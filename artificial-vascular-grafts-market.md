<p><p>Artificial vascular grafts are medical devices used in vascular surgery to bypass or replace diseased or damaged blood vessels. These grafts are made from synthetic materials such as polyethylene terephthalate (PET) or expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (ePTFE), and are used to restore blood flow in patients with blocked or damaged arteries.</p><p>The global artificial vascular grafts market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing prevalence of vascular diseases, rising geriatric population, and advancements in surgical techniques. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.7% during the forecast period. Other factors contributing to market growth include the increasing demand for minimally invasive surgeries, growing healthcare infrastructure in emerging economies, and a rising number of cardiovascular surgeries.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the artificial vascular grafts market is the development of bioengineered grafts using tissue engineering techniques. These grafts have the potential to mimic the structure and function of natural blood vessels, reducing the risk of complications such as thrombosis or infection. Additionally, technological advancements such as the use of nanotechnology and 3D printing in manufacturing artificial vascular grafts are further driving market growth and innovation.</p></p>
