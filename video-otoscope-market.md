<p><p>Video otoscopes are devices used by medical professionals to examine the ear canal and tympanic membrane. They come in various types based on the output they provide. S-Video, Composite, HDMI, DVI, and USB video otoscopes differ in the type of video output they offer. S-Video and Composite otoscopes provide standard definition video output, while HDMI and DVI otoscopes provide high-definition video output. USB otoscopes allow for easy connectivity with computers and other devices for viewing and storing images and videos captured during examinations.</p></p>
