<p><p>Arthroscopy cannulas are categorized as either reusable or disposable based on their usage. Reusable cannulas are designed for multiple uses, requiring proper sterilization between procedures. Disposable cannulas, on the other hand, are meant for single-use only and are discarded after each procedure to prevent cross-contamination. Both types are used in arthroscopic surgeries to provide access for instruments and thorough examination of joint structures. The choice between reusable and disposable cannulas depends on individual preferences, cost considerations, and infection control protocols.</p></p>
