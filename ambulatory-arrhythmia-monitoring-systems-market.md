<p><p>External ambulatory arrhythmia monitoring systems are portable devices that are worn by the patient to continuously monitor heart activity. These systems are easy to use and provide real-time data on irregular heart rhythms. On the other hand, implantable monitoring systems are devices that are surgically implanted inside the body to monitor heart activity for an extended period of time. Implantable systems are preferred for patients who require long-term monitoring or have frequent episodes of arrhythmias. Both types of monitoring systems play a significant role in diagnosing and managing heart rhythm disorders.</p></p>
