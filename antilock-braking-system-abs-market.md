<p><p>Antilock Braking System (ABS) is a safety feature that prevents wheels from locking up during braking, allowing the driver to maintain steering control. The market is divided into different types based on the number of channels used in the system. One-channel ABS uses a single control channel for all four wheels, while two-channel ABS has separate channels for the front and rear wheels. Three-channel ABS adds a separate channel for one of the rear wheels, and four-channel ABS provides individual control for each wheel, offering the highest level of safety and control.</p></p>
