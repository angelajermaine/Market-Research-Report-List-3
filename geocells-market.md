High-density polyethylene geocells are known for their flexibility and durability, making them ideal for applications where stability and strength are required. On the other hand, polypropylene geocells are lightweight and resistant to chemicals, making them suitable for areas with high water exposure. Both types of geocells are widely used in various industries such as construction, landscaping, and erosion control due to their effectiveness in soil stabilization and reinforcement.</p></p>
