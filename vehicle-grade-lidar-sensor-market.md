<p><p>Vehicle grade LiDAR sensors are classified into two main types: mechanical LiDAR and solid-state LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR sensors use moving parts such as mirrors or rotating lasers to scan the environment, while solid-state LiDAR sensors utilize semiconductor components for scanning. Mechanical LiDAR sensors are traditionally more common due to their longer range and higher resolution, but solid-state LiDAR sensors are gaining popularity for their smaller size, lower cost, and improved reliability. Both types play a crucial role in enabling autonomous driving technology.</p></p>
