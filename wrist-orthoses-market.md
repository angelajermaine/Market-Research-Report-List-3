<p><p>Wrist orthoses can be divided into two main categories: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support while allowing for some degree of movement and flexibility in the wrist joint. These are often used during rehabilitation or for conditions that require controlled movement. Static orthotics, on the other hand, are designed to restrict movement in the wrist joint to provide maximum support and stability. These are typically used for conditions that require immobilization or protection of the wrist.</p></p>
