<p><p>Flu RNA vaccines can be categorized into different types based on their mechanism of action. Non-replicating mRNA vaccines deliver mRNA into cells to produce viral proteins for an immune response. In Vivo Self-Replicating mRNA vaccines replicate within the body to continuously produce antigens. In Vitro Dendritic Cell Non-Replicating mRNA Vaccines involve dendritic cells processing mRNA to trigger an immune response. Other types of flu RNA vaccines may utilize different approaches for delivering viral genetic material and stimulating immunity.</p></p>
