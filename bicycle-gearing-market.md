<p><strong>The Bicycle Gearing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>External(Derailleur)</li><li>Internal(Hub)</li><li>Internal(Bottom Bracket)</li><li>Internal and External Combined</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>External Bicycle Gearing refers to the traditional derailleur system where gears are shifted externally through the use of a derailleur mechanism. Internal Hub Gearing involves gears being enclosed within the rear hub of the wheel, providing a clean and low-maintenance option. Internal Bottom Bracket Gearing integrates gears within the bottom bracket area of the bike frame. Internal and External Combined Gearing combines both internal hub and external derailleur systems for a versatile gear range. Other types of gearing include electronic shifting systems, belt drives, and continuously variable transmissions.</p></p>
